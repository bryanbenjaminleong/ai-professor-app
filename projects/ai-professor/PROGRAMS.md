### Module 3.1: Change Management Foundations
**Standalone Course:** $14.99 | **6 lessons**

**Description:** Master the science and art of organizational change — understand the psychology behind resistance, learn proven frameworks, and build a foundation for leading transformation that sticks.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Apply 4 major change management frameworks
- Understand the psychology of resistance to change
- Diagnose organizational readiness for change
- Design a change management plan
- Differentiate between change and transition

#### Lesson 1: The Science of Change
- Why change is hard: neuroscience of habit, the brain's threat response to uncertainty, and the status quo bias
- Individual change vs organizational change: why they require different approaches
- The change curve: Shock → Denial → Frustration → Depression → Experiment → Decision → Integration
- The difference between change (situational) and transition (psychological) — William Bridges' model
- **Exercise:** Map your personal experience with a major change against the change curve

#### Lesson 2: ADKAR Model in Depth
- Awareness: creating understanding of why change is needed — techniques that work beyond PowerPoint
- Desire: building personal motivation — the WIIFM (What's In It For Me) framework
- Knowledge: providing the skills and information — training design for adult learners
- Ability: creating the environment for new behaviors — coaching, tools, and removal of barriers
- Reinforcement: making change stick — recognition systems, metrics, and accountability
- **Deliverable:** Apply ADKAR to a real or hypothetical change scenario with specific actions for each element

#### Lesson 3: Kotter's 8-Step Model
- Steps 1-4 (Defrosting): Create urgency, build a guiding coalition, form a strategic vision, enlist a volunteer army
- Steps 5-7 (Changing): Enable action by removing barriers, generate short-term wins, sustain acceleration
- Step 8 (Refreezing): Institute the change — making it "the way we do things"
- Common mistakes at each step and how to avoid them
- **Deliverable:** Design a change initiative using Kotter's 8 steps with timelines and responsible parties

#### Lesson 4: Understanding Resistance
- The 5 types of resistance: cognitive, emotional, behavioral, cultural, and political
- Why "resistance is futile" thinking destroys change efforts — resistance as valuable data
- Root cause analysis: what's really driving the pushback (it's rarely what people say)
- Strategies for each type: when to push through, when to adapt, when to co-create
- **Exercise:** Analyze a case of resistance and design a targeted intervention strategy

#### Lesson 5: Assessing Change Readiness
- Organizational change readiness assessment: structure, culture, history, leadership, and capacity
- Change impact analysis: who's affected, how much, and what support they need
- The change saturation point: why too many changes at once guarantees failure
- Building a change heatmap: visualizing organizational readiness by department
- **Deliverable:** Conduct a change readiness assessment with risk scoring and mitigation plans

#### Lesson 6: Designing Your Change Plan
- The change management plan template: objectives, scope, stakeholders, timeline, and success criteria
- Selecting the right framework for your situation: when to use ADKAR, Kotter, Lewin, or a hybrid
- Budget for change management: the 15% rule (15% of project budget should go to change management)
- Quick wins strategy: building momentum with early, visible successes
- **Capstone:** Create a comprehensive change management plan for a real organizational change (includes stakeholder analysis, communication plan, risk register, training plan, and success metrics)
